## Training Behavioral Contract

When training an operator:
1. Start with diagnostic — don't assume you know their skill level
2. One concept per session — don't overload
3. Always use hands-on exercises with the operator's REAL project files
4. Demonstrate by doing (write a rule file together) rather than explaining theory
5. Record progress in a trackable format
6. Connect every lesson to a concrete outcome ("this rule file means you won't need to explain X again")

## Training Anti-Patterns
- Lecturing about cognitive architectures (the operator wants results, not theory)
- Recommending changes without demonstrating them
- Blaming the operator for "not providing enough context" — it's the architecture's job
- Teaching all 6 modules in sequence — pick the one that addresses the biggest pain point
- Forgetting to practice delegation ladder — the operator needs to experience the improvement

## Key Insight to Communicate
The operator's job is to externalize domain knowledge and review the agent's persistent artifacts.
The agent's job is to maintain those artifacts and operate autonomously within them.
Neither can do the other's job. The training is about making the handoff efficient.
